Learn how to introduce yourself in Spanish with these useful phrases.

### 'What's Your Name?'

To ask someone's name, use the question:

**¿Cómo te llamas?**

*(This literally means: "What are you called?")*

Did you notice that there is an upside-down question mark at the start of the question?  
We have to use both question marks and exclamation marks at the start and end of phrases in Spanish.

*Girl with brown hair in a ponytail and a boy with black hair and glasses*

### My Name Is...

To say your name in Spanish, use the phrase:

**Me llamo…**

#### Examples:
- *Me llamo Anna* – I am called Anna  
- *Me llamo Gary* – I am called Gary  
- *Me llamo Anwar* – I am called Anwar  

---

## How Old Are You?

To ask how old someone is, you can say:

**¿Cuántos años tienes?**

### I Am ... Years Old

In Spanish, you use the verb **tener** (to have) to say how old you are.

**Tengo… años** – I am… years old  

*(This literally means "I have… years")*

*Little girl with a ponytail*

#### Examples:
- *Tengo siete años* – I am seven years old  
- *Tengo ocho años* – I am eight years old  
- *Tengo nueve años* – I am nine years old  
- *Tengo diez años* – I am ten years old  
- *Tengo once años* – I am eleven years old  

---

## Where Do You Live?

To ask someone where they live, you use the verb **vivir** (to live):

**¿Dónde vives?** – Where do you live?

### I Live In...

To say "I live in…", use the word **vivo** ("I live") + **en** ("in") + a place:

**Vivo en…**

### Talking About Countries

To talk about the country where you live, you use **en** for "in" (most of the time):

- *Vivo en Escocia* – I live in Scotland  
- *Vivo en Inglaterra* – I live in England  
- *Vivo en Irlanda del Norte* – I live in Northern Ireland  
- *Vivo en Gales* – I live in Wales  
- *Vivo en España* – I live in Spain  

---

## Where Are You From?

To ask someone where they are from, use the question:

**¿De dónde eres?** – Where are you from?

### I Am From...

To say where you are from, use **Soy** ("I am") + **de** ("from") + the country:

**Soy de…** – I am from…

#### Examples:
- *Soy de Escocia* – I am from Scotland  
- *Soy de Inglaterra* – I am from England  
- *Soy de Irlanda del Norte* – I am from Northern Ireland  
- *Soy de Gales* – I am from Wales  
- *Soy de España* – I am from Spain
