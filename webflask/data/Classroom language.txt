Learn How to Talk About School in Spanish

## Classroom Instructions
Here are some useful instructions that you might hear your teacher use in class:

- **Escuchad** - Listen
- **Mirad** - Look
- **Sentaos** - Sit down
- **Levantaos** - Stand up
- **Silencio por favor** - Silence please
- **Levantad la mano** - Put your hands up
- **Repetid** - Repeat

*(These are all written in the plural form of the verb which you use when talking to more than one person.)*

### 'Do you have...?'
To ask a person if they have something, you can say:

**¿Tienes…?** - Do you have…?

You can then add the noun (the thing) you want to know about.

**Examples:**

- ¿Tienes una goma? - Do you have a rubber?
- ¿Tienes un lápiz? - Do you have a pencil?

*("Tienes" comes from the verb "tener," which means 'to have.')*

![Image](/static/4b.png)

### 'I have...'
To talk about what you have or don't have, you can say:

- **Tengo…** - I have…
- **No tengo…** - I don’t have…

**Examples:**

- Tengo una goma - I have a rubber
- No tengo un lápiz - I don’t have a pencil

### 'Today I have...'
To say which school subjects you are doing today, you can say:

**Hoy tengo…** - Today I have…

**Example:**

Hoy tengo las matemáticas, el inglés y la historia - Today I have maths, English, and history.

## Favourite Subjects
To ask someone what their favourite subject is, you can say:

**¿Cuál es tu asignatura favorita?** - What is your favourite subject?

![Image](/static/4c.png)

To say what your favourite subject is, you can use:

**Mi asignatura favorita es…** - My favourite subject is…

**Example:**

Mi asignatura favorita es español - My favourite subject is Spanish.
